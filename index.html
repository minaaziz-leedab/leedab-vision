The strategic lens

apply to every roadmap decision
1
Vault sovereignty is the moat
Not the agents. Not the voice. The defensible thing is "your business runs on a brain you own". Lean in.
2
Contracts not vibes
Define LeedAB's own interfaces day one. OpenClaw is one implementation, not the truth. Or "movable" becomes a 4-month rewrite.
3
Voice is last for the business
Terminal works → that's the sale. Aria is icing. Personal Aria continues; biz Aria deferred until paying customers are stable.
4
Per-business vault is ops weight + a feature
100 customers = 100 vaults to manage. Price it in. Upside: data portability is a sales line — they leave with their brain.
5
Observability from day one
Voice → COO → agent → vault → n8n is undebuggable without tracing. Build it before customer five, not after.
6
Triage > ambition
What's the minimum end-to-end demo? Not 27 agents — maybe four. Smaller is faster to truth. Cut anything that doesn't close a deal.
7
Defend the positioning
Operator with hands is crowded. Differentiator: per-business + local + vault-owned. Three words tight.

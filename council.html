SM
Member 02
The sceptical SMB buyer
Logistics ops manager, time-poor, burnt twice
Hears "AI agent" 5 times a week. Burnt by SaaS lock-in twice already. Has zero patience for hype.

Two problems hit me the moment I read the pitch.

First: "Mac Mini ships to your premises" — who installs it on my office network? My nephew? An onsite tech costs me $400 a day; if LeedAB doesn't include white-glove install in the price, the appliance story is a non-starter for shops without an IT person.

Second: $1,800/month for a 30-person op gets compared directly to my Xero + ServiceM8 + Sage stack, which I already trust and have integrated payroll on. The "their data, locally" line is reassuring but I've heard variations of that promise from three vendors who later got acquired or pivoted.

Vendor-risk objection that has no sharp answer in the deck: "If you go bust in 18 months, what does my hand-over actually look like — contractually, who keeps the Mini, who supports it, and is there an escrow on the source code?" The current "you keep your data" answer doesn't address operational continuity, only data portability.
EN
Member 03
The technical-architecture critic
Staff engineer, has shipped at scale
Knows the gap between "designed" and "shipped". Looking for the ADR most likely to need replacement and the over-confident time estimate.

ADR-002 (OpenClaw + contracts-not-vibes) is the riskiest call — contracts as escape hatches only work if you've actually built against a second runtime, and you haven't, so the abstraction is theoretical and will rot under shipping pressure.

ADR-006 (manifest reorg) is in flight but I cannot find a schema-migration story for live customers when the canonical manifest schema changes in v1.1 — that gap will become an operational nightmare at customer #5 onward.

The single-bot routing in ADR-001 budgets 90 seconds total wall-clock; under realistic Anthropic API tail latency (p95 ~5–8s, p99 ~15s+) you will hit the 90s ceiling on 5–10% of orchestrations once production load hits, and the "fallback: looking into it" guarantee turns into a customer-trust problem.

Most over-confident claim in the build plan: Q3 2026 "Mac Mini physical pilots with 1-2 customers" depends on manifest reorg + COO orchestrator + Champion all shipping by August — three load-bearing things shipping concurrently is the standard recipe for slipping a quarter.
CA
Member 04
The competitive analyst
Maps the AI-agent market for a living
Knows what's shipping and what's vapor. Looking for sharp competitor differentiation and the open-source threat.

Closest direct competitor pitching local-first AI for SMBs right now is Sintra — they've shipped a multi-agent product to thousands of SMBs, their pricing ladder is public, and their go-to-market is studyable. You don't mention them anywhere in the pitch.

Lindy is the most-funded multi-agent platform in 2026; cloud-hosted (your differentiator) but their pricing pressure will define the market's expectation.

CrewAI + Ollama on a customer's existing Mac is a real threat for technical SMBs — zero cost, full control, no vendor risk. LeedAB's defence is "non-technical SMBs don't ship CrewAI" which is true today but the gap closes every six months as the agent frameworks mature.

One-line answer to "why won't OpenAI / Anthropic just build this?": "OpenAI does not ship Mac Minis. The local-first hardware-aware pitch is structurally inconvenient for cloud-first incumbents — they would cannibalise their compute margin." Defensible — until one of them shifts strategy. Anthropic specifically (Computer Use product) has shown signs of caring about the local-execution surface.
